## Overview

A nine-microcredential certificate program structured around the productive tension between cultural processes and institutional resources — and the shared values that can resolve that tension through AI-augmented creative practice.

Completion of the nine-microcredential certificate certifies foundational literacy in cultural production, institutional mediation, platform and workflow economics, multimodal experience design, and AI-augmented creative practice formation — competencies that are typically assumed but not formally taught in senior undergraduate and graduate courses in arts, culture, creative industries, and design entrepreneurship.

---

## Architecture

The certificate is organized as three stacks of three microcredentials each. Each stack operates at a different scale and addresses a distinct dimension of creative industries work.

### Stack 1 — Cultural Processes (MC1–MC3)
*Local scale. Readiness for applied cultural analysis.*  
Explores cultural production as it is lived and practiced — often in tension with the institutions that surround it.

Prepares students for: individual voice, production literacy, platform economics, and comfort with case studies and abstraction.

### Stack 2 — Institutional Resources (MC4–MC6)
*Global/institutional scale. Readiness for advanced mediation.*  
Examines how institutions manage, fund, distribute, and mediate culture — often in tension with the organic needs of cultural producers.

Prepares students for: archival work, experience design, communities of practice, workflow and broadcasting economics.

### Stack 3 — Shared Values (MC7–MC9)
*Operational scale. Readiness for practice founding and client development.*  
Attempts to resolve the tensions between Stacks 1 and 2 through AI-augmented practice formation, IP strategy, and community/client development.

Prepares students for: founding or joining an AI-augmented creative studio, navigating IP and licensing, and building sustainable client relationships.

---

## Institutional Pitch

This program is designed to be:

- **Additive, not competitive** — it does not replace degree programs
- **Preparatory, not substitutive** — it feeds senior and graduate courses
- **Modular and portable** — each stack is independently licensable; case examples can be localized by institution

The dialectical structure (cultural processes → institutional resources → shared values) gives the certificate a theoretical spine that faculty in arts management, cultural policy, and creative industries will recognize and respect.
