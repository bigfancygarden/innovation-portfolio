# MICROCREDENTIAL 1: PERSONA, VOICE, AND CULTURAL IDENTITY

## YOU® Audio Essay / Proto-Podcast

**Derived from**: Cultural Icons & Popular Art (IAT 203, 2002-2006)  
**Format**: Self-paced/asynchronous (individual)  
**Platform**: Soundtrap for Education  
**Estimated Duration**: 3-6 weeks (flexible)  
**Credit Hours**: 1.5 CEUs (Continuing Education Units)

---

## Course Description

This microcredential examines the construction of persona as a cultural object, with particular attention to voice as a medium of authority and authenticity. Students develop and produce an audio essay that functions as a spoken extension of a personal brand concept (YOU®), exploring how identity, performance, and mediation operate through sound.

**Podcasting is framed explicitly as an emergent audio form**—not as a mature platformed medium—positioning the work at the threshold before platform-mediated identity, algorithmic distribution, and parasocial audio publics became infrastructural.

---

## Historical Context: The Time Capsule Frame

This microcredential is derived from a course taught 2002-2006 at SFU's School of Interactive Arts & Technology, **frozen at 2006** just before three fundamental ruptures restructured cultural production:

1. **Podcasting** → Platform-mediated identity (before voice became ambient/infrastructural)
2. **Social media** → Ubiquitous algorithmic mediation (before feed logic replaced human navigation)
3. **Artificial Intelligence** → Synthetic/generative media (before generative production without human authorship)

**This positioning is intentional**. By working within pre-platform logic, you'll experience what changed when these technologies emerged, enabling reflexive analysis of contemporary cultural conditions.

---

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion, students will be able to:

1. **Analyze identity as a culturally constructed artifact** rather than essential self-expression
2. **Translate a conceptual persona across media** (from visual/textual branding to audio representation)
3. **Use voice reflectively as a representational tool** (understanding tone, pacing, intimacy, performance)
4. **Apply cultural theory to personal branding practices** (drawing on Benjamin, Baudrillard, Seabrook, Eco)
5. **Produce a structured audio essay** using Soundtrap for Education as a compositional environment

## Course Structure (Self-Paced Modules)

### Module 1: Cultural Theory & Identity Construction (Week 1)

**Core Concepts**:
- Aura and authenticity (Walter Benjamin)
- Hyperreality and simulation (Jean Baudrillard)
- Nobrow culture (John Seabrook)
- Persona as cultural object

**Readings**:
- Benjamin, "The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction" (excerpts)
- Baudrillard, "Simulacra and Simulation" (excerpts)
- Seabrook, "Nobrow: The Culture of Marketing and the Marketing of Culture" (excerpts)

**Activities**:
- Reflective journal: What makes a persona "authentic"?
- Case study analysis: Examine 2-3 public figures as constructed personas
- Discussion forum: YOU® as satire vs. YOU® as prototype

**Deliverable**: 250-word concept statement identifying core YOU® brand attributes

---

### Module 2: Voice as Medium (Week 2)

**Core Concepts**:
- Voice vs. text vs. image
- Intimacy and distance in audio media
- Parasociality before platforms
- Rhetorical stance and vocal presence

**Readings**:
- Selected essays on radio, audio documentary, spoken-word criticism
- Early podcast examples (2005-2006 era)

**Activities**:
- Listen to 3-5 audio essays/proto-podcasts
- Analyze: How does voice change the persona presentation?
- Experiment: Record 2-3 minute voice memo exploring different vocal approaches

**Deliverable**: 300-word analysis of how voice will shape YOUR YOU® brand differently than visual/textual media

---

### Module 3: Soundtrap Production Fundamentals (Week 3)

**Technical Skills**:
- Soundtrap interface navigation
- Recording in Soundtrap (browser-based DAW)
- Basic editing (cut, trim, fade)
- Mixing fundamentals (levels, panning)
- Adding music/sound effects (optional)

**Activities**:
- Soundtrap tutorial completion
- Practice recording: Record same 1-minute script 3 different ways (different tone/energy)
- Peer feedback: Share practice recording in discussion forum

**Deliverable**: 3-5 minute rough draft of audio essay concept

---

### Module 4: Narrative Structure & Audio Composition (Week 4)

**Core Concepts**:
- Audio essay structure (intro → body → conclusion)
- Sonic composition (silence, pacing, rhythm)
- Performance vs. authenticity
- Editing as part of self-construction

**Activities**:
- Outline YOUR audio essay structure
- Draft script (even if you'll speak extemporaneously, outline key points)
- Production: Record full audio essay in Soundtrap

**Deliverable**: First complete draft (8-12 minutes) uploaded to Soundtrap, shared for peer review

---

### Module 5: Refinement & Critical Reflection (Weeks 5-6)

**Activities**:
- Peer review: Listen to 2-3 other students' drafts, provide constructive feedback
- Revise YOUR audio essay based on feedback
- Write critical reflection paper

**Deliverable**: Final audio essay + written reflection

## Final Deliverables

### 1. YOU® Audio Essay / Proto-Podcast

**Specifications**:
- **Duration**: 8-12 minutes
- **Format**: MP3 exported from Soundtrap
- **Content**: Spoken extension of YOUR YOU® brand, demonstrating:
  - Clear articulation of brand identity
  - Thoughtful use of voice as representational medium
  - Coherent narrative structure
  - Conscious use of tone, pacing, vocal presence

**Production notes**:
- Solo voice or lightly produced (music optional, not required)
- Editing encouraged; elaborate production not required
- Focus on content and voice, not technical complexity
- **Not intended for public release** (closed studio environment, not platform)

---

### 2. Written Context & Reflection

**Specifications**:
- **Length**: 750-1000 words
- **Format**: PDF or Word document

**Required content**:
1. **How working in Soundtrap shaped your use of voice**
   - What did the platform enable or constrain?
   - How did cloud-based composition affect your process?

2. **How audio altered the meaning of YOUR YOU® brand**
   - What was gained compared to visual/textual formats?
   - What was lost?
   - How does voice change authenticity/performance?

3. **Critical analysis of mediation and performance**
   - How does voice function as cultural signal?
   - How is intimacy constructed without audience feedback?
   - How does pre-platform podcasting differ from contemporary podcast culture?

4. **Cultural theory integration**
   - Explicitly connect YOUR work to at least TWO course concepts:
     - Aura and presence (Benjamin)
     - Hyperreality and simulation (Baudrillard)
     - Performance and mediation
     - Representation vs. authenticity
     - Persona as cultural object (YOU®)

## Explicit Temporal Framing

**This microcredential treats podcasting as an emergent audio form**, not as a mature political or platformed medium. 

**Students should NOT model their work on contemporary podcast genres** that presume:
- Audience capture and growth
- Algorithmic recommendation and discovery
- Branding funnels and monetization
- Platform metrics (followers, downloads, likes)

**Instead, draw inspiration from**:
- Radio essays (NPR, BBC, CBC)
- Audio documentaries
- Public lectures and recorded talks
- Spoken-word criticism

**This constraint is intentional and methodological**. It allows you to experience podcasting at its **point of emergence**—before it consolidated into platform-driven, personality-centric infrastructure.

## Post-Assignment Reflexive Prompt (Optional)

After completing YOUR audio essay, consider these questions:

1. **How would this work differently if you were producing for Spotify, Apple Podcasts, or YouTube?**
   - What would you change to optimize for discovery/engagement?
   - How would platform metrics shape your creative decisions?

2. **How does working in Soundtrap's closed environment differ from platform publishing?**
   - What's gained by NOT releasing publicly?
   - What's lost?

3. **If YOU had access to AI voice generation, how would that change this assignment?**
   - Would YOU still use your own voice?
   - How would synthetic voice affect authenticity?

**These questions are not graded**, but they invite you to contrast YOUR proto-podcast experience with contemporary podcast culture—making visible what changed when voice became platformized and algorithmically distributed.
