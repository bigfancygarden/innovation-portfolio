# MICROCREDENTIAL 2: CULTURAL SYSTEMS, ICONS, AND EXPERIENTIAL DESIGN

## Curated Event / Installation / Virtual Environment

**Derived from**: Cultural Icons & Popular Art (IAT 203, 2002-2006)  
**Format**: 6-7 weeks, structured cohort (remote teams)  
**Platform**: Miro (primary collaboration), Google Workspace (documentation)  
**Meeting Schedule**: Weekly synchronous sessions + asynchronous team work  
**Credit Hours**: 3.0 CEUs (Continuing Education Units)

---

## Course Description

This microcredential centers on culture as a system, emphasizing spatialization of meaning, curatorial authority, and experiential design as cultural argument. Working in remote teams, students analyze cultural icons as systems (not isolated objects) and design spatial or experiential environments that translate cultural meaning into coordinated encounters.

**The course uses ARIES (Autopoietic Remix for Intertextual Ecological Systems) methodology**, which coordinates cultural production across three layers:
1. **Cultural Reservoir** (Intertextual Inputs) - canonical texts, practices, iconographies
2. **Experiential Assembly** (Remix Operations) - spatial, temporal, atmospheric orchestration
3. **Value Objectification** (Authorization Layer) - licenses, permissions, revenue models

---

## Historical Context: The Time Capsule Frame

This microcredential is derived from a course taught 2002-2006 at SFU's School of Interactive Arts & Technology, **frozen at 2006** just before three fundamental ruptures restructured cultural production:

1. **Podcasting** → Platform-mediated identity
2. **Social media** → Ubiquitous algorithmic mediation
3. **Artificial Intelligence** → Synthetic/generative media

**This positioning is intentional**. The course operates as a **methodological time capsule**, allowing reflexive analysis of how these technologies changed cultural authority, circulation, and production.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion, students will be able to:

1. **Treat cultural icons as systems, not objects** (networks of meanings, not isolated texts)
2. **Design spatial or experiential arguments** that translate cultural theory into curated encounters

4. **Work collaboratively on complex cultural artifacts** in remote, distributed teams
5. **Apply ARIES methodology** across all three coordinated layers

## The Pedagogical Structure: Casablanca → Fionn MacCool's

### Casablanca Pre-Visualization (Exemplar)

**Function**: Teaching artifact showing HOW ARIES works

**Students analyze the Casablanca pre-viz PDF to understand**:
- How film iconography (Cultural Reservoir) becomes event design
- How iconic scenes translate to spatial moments (Experiential Assembly)
- How licensing/permissions shape feasibility (Value Objectification)

**Key learning**:
- *Casablanca* as hyperreal cultural node (Eco: "clichés talking among themselves")
- Rick's Café mythology as atmospheric blueprint
- Film screening rights + venue licensing as design constraints
- Culinary translation (Moroccan cuisine, gin joints)

**This is the METHOD students will apply to their own context**

---

### Fionn MacCool's World Cup (Application)

**Function**: Students APPLY same ARIES methodology to different context

**Same location, different cultural frame**:
- **Location**: Fionn MacCool's, False Creek, Vancouver
- **Shift**: From Anthony Bourdain celebration/fundraiser → Soccer/World Cup revenue generation
- **Challenge**: How does same venue support different cultural programming?

**Students design pre-visualizations showing**:
- How Irish pub + Vancouver Whitecaps + World Cup 2026 iconographies coordinate
- How False Creek location (BC Place proximity, TELUS Science World, urban context) enables multi-venue possibilities
- How licensing complexity (broadcast rights, sponsorships, music, food/beverage) shapes revenue models
- How seasonal off-season programming (winter World Cup) creates new value proposition

## Course Content by Week

### Week 1: Introduction + ARIES Framework

**Synchronous Session** (2 hours):
- Course overview and learning outcomes
- ARIES methodology introduction (three coordinated layers)
- Time capsule framing (2002-2006 context, three ruptures)
- Team formation process
- Introduce Casablanca pre-viz PDF

**Asynchronous Work**:
- Read Casablanca pre-viz PDF thoroughly
- Self-assess: What skills/interests do you bring to team?
- Initial team formation (via discussion forum or Miro board)

**Deliverable**: Team Contract
- Team member roles and responsibilities
- Communication protocols (Slack/Discord/email)
- Meeting schedule (when will team meet outside class?)
- Conflict resolution process
- Signatures (digital)

---

### Week 2: ARIES Layer 1 - Cultural Reservoir (Intertextual Inputs)

**Synchronous Session** (2 hours):
- Deep dive: Analyzing Casablanca's Cultural Reservoir
  - Film iconography (scenes, characters, dialogue)
  - WWII nostalgia and romantic mythology
  - Rick's Café as archetypal space
- Intertextuality (Eco): How cultural icons accumulate meaning through circulation
- Assignment: Map YOUR Cultural Reservoir for Fionn MacCool's World Cup

**Asynchronous Work**:
**Teams research and document**:
1. **Irish Pub Culture**
   - Iconography, atmosphere, social rituals
   - Food/drink traditions
   - Music and storytelling customs

2. **Soccer/World Cup 2026**
   - Global tournament mythology
   - Vancouver Whitecaps identity
   - Fan culture and viewing rituals
   - 2026 specific context (North American hosting)

3. **False Creek Urban Context**
   - BC Place stadium (capacity, events, adjacency)
   - TELUS Science World (potential partnership?)
   - Waterfront/seawall (pedestrian flow)
   - Transit access (SkyTrain)

4. **Fionn MacCool's as Venue**
   - Current operations (capacity, layout, branding)
   - Existing events/programming
   - Ownership/management structure
   - Opportunities and constraints

**Deliverable**: Cultural Inputs Inventory (Miro board or Google Doc)
- Comprehensive list of cultural references, icons, practices
- Images, quotes, atmosphere descriptors
- Research citations
- Team commentary on which inputs are most promising

---

### Week 3: ARIES Layer 2 - Experiential Assembly (Remix Operations)

**Synchronous Session** (2 hours):
- Deep dive: Analyzing Casablanca's Experiential Assembly
  - How scenes become spatial moments
  - Temporal sequencing (arrival → immersion → reflection)
  - Atmospheric coordination (lighting, sound, scent, taste)
- Spatial design principles for hospitality venues
- Assignment: Design YOUR event flow for Fionn MacCool's

**Asynchronous Work**:
**Teams design experiential components**:

1. **Spatial Staging**
   - Floor plan modifications (if any)
   - Sightlines for screen viewing
   - Standing vs. seated areas
   - Bar flow and service logistics

2. **Atmospheric Orchestration**
   - Lighting design (match viewing + pub ambiance)
   - Sound design (broadcast audio + venue playlist)
   - Scent (food, beer, atmosphere)
   - Decor (flags, banners, memorabilia)

3. **Temporal Sequencing**
   - Pre-match (arrival, settling in, atmosphere building)
   - Match viewing (engagement, peaks, breaks)
   - Post-match (celebration or commiseration, transition)
   - Multi-match events (tournament progression)

4. **Culinary Translation**
   - Food offerings (Irish pub staples + international fan foods)
   - Beverage program (beer, cocktails, non-alcoholic)
   - Service model (table service, counter ordering, food trucks?)

**Deliverable**: Draft Event Design (Miro board + Google Slides)
- Event flow diagram (timeline)
- Spatial layout sketch
- Mood boards (atmosphere, decor, vibe)
- Sample playlist (music selection)
- Menu concepts

---

### Week 4: ARIES Layer 3 - Value Objectification (Authorization Layer)

**Synchronous Session** (2 hours):
- Deep dive: Analyzing Casablanca's licensing/permissions structure
  - Film screening rights
  - Music licensing ("As Time Goes By")
  - Venue permits
  - Cost implications
- Introduction to licensing tiers
  - Educational fair use vs. commercial application
  - Creative Commons vs. commercial licenses
  - Example: Karaoke-Version.com tiered pricing model
- Revenue model frameworks
- Assignment: Build YOUR authorization matrix

**Asynchronous Work**:
**Teams research and document**:

1. **Broadcast Rights**
   - World Cup broadcast licensing (FIFA, TSN, Sportsnet?)
   - Public performance licensing for venue viewing
   - Costs per match vs. tournament package

2. **Music Licensing**
   - SOCAN (Society of Composers, Authors and Music Publishers of Canada)
   - Venue music licensing requirements
   - Curated playlist clearances

3. **Venue Operating Permits**
   - Liquor licensing capacity limits
   - Special event permits
   - Extended hours permissions
   - Food service health permits

4. **Sponsorship Opportunities**
   - TELUS (potential major sponsor? Connect to Blueprint 2022-2023 work)
   - Beer companies (official World Cup sponsors vs. local breweries)
   - Vancouver Whitecaps partnership
   - Tourism Vancouver

5. **Revenue Model**
   - Ticketing (cover charge per match? Tournament passes?)
   - Food/beverage sales projections
   - Sponsorship tiers
   - Merchandise possibilities

**Deliverable**: Authorization Matrix + Revenue Model (Google Sheets + Slides)
- Comprehensive licensing requirements with cost estimates
- Revenue projections (conservative, moderate, optimistic)
- Sponsorship tier proposals
- Feasibility assessment (is this actually viable?)

## Explicit Temporal Framing

**Same principle as Microcredential 1**: This course operates within pre-platform, pre-algorithm, pre-AI cultural logic.

**You are designing experiences that assume**:
- Human curation and authority (not algorithmic recommendation)
- Intentional cultural references (not generative production)
- Physical/spatial encounter (not virtual/metaverse)

**This framing allows you to**:
- Understand what changed when platforms/algorithms/AI emerged
- Design with conscious intentionality about mediation
- Treat cultural icons as systems requiring human interpretation

**Post-assignment reflexive questions** (not graded):
- How would social media change YOUR event design?
- How would AI-generated content affect YOUR cultural references?
- How would algorithmic audience targeting change YOUR approach?
